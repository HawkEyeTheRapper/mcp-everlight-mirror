The Amazon RME pre-employment assessment consists of two sections; mechanical reasoning and workstyle.
 
Mechanical Reasoning Assessment
The mechanical reasoning assessment measures your ability to learn how to operate, maintain, install, and repair equipment and machinery and apply physical and mechanical concepts to solve a given problem. Questions are presented in the form of diagrams and illustrations, with one correct answer out of several multiple-choice options. To help you prepare, we’ve outlined the most common mechanical reasoning topics below.
Levers
Questions relating to levers test your understanding of the relationship between the input force and output force, and how this is used to gain a mechanical advantage.
Pulleys
Much like levers, questions relating to pulleys test your understanding of how much force is required to move a specified weight, but by applying a different concept.
Gears
A gear system has multiple uses. It can force motion, cause a directional shift, or alter the speed.
Force and Motion
These questions test your knowledge of acceleration, pressure, and the principles of gravity.
Energy
As with force and motion, the questions that fall under this category can be very broad in subject matter and may relate to magnetic, potential, thermal, or kinetic energy.
Simple Electrical Circuits
Here you’ll need to demonstrate knowledge of how electricity flows through a circuit.
 
Workstyle Assessment:
The workstyle assessment provides valuable insight into how comfortable a person will feel within a particular role, otherwise known as “job fit.” Your responses come together to create a unique profile.Similarly, there are no “good” or “bad” traits -some traits and profiles are simply more associated with success for particular roles.The workstyle assessment determines if you'd be happy and comfortable in a position long-term.
