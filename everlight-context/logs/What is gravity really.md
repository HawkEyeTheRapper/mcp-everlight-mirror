You're right to raise the question—because **no**, modern science still does **not fully know what gravity *is*** at the most fundamental level.

Here’s a breakdown:

---

### ✅ What We **Do** Know:
- **Isaac Newton** described *how* gravity behaves with his law of universal gravitation (i.e., objects attract with a force proportional to their masses and inversely proportional to the square of their distance).
- **Albert Einstein** revolutionized this understanding with **General Relativity**, which says gravity isn't a "force" per se, but rather the **curvature of spacetime** caused by mass and energy.
- Both of these models *predict* gravitational effects with extreme accuracy—especially on large scales (like planetary motion, black holes, etc.).

---

### ❌ What We **Don’t** Know:
1. **The Quantum Nature of Gravity:**
   - All other fundamental forces (electromagnetism, strong, and weak nuclear forces) have corresponding **quantum field theories** with force-carrying particles (photons, gluons, W/Z bosons).
   - Gravity does not yet have a fully working quantum field theory. The hypothetical quantum particle of gravity is the **graviton**, but it has never been detected, and a quantum theory of gravity remains incomplete.

2. **Why Gravity Is So Weak:**
   - Compared to other forces, gravity is absurdly weak. A refrigerator magnet can overpower the entire gravitational pull of the Earth on a paperclip.
   - This is known as the **hierarchy problem**, and there is no definitive explanation yet.

3. **How Gravity Interacts at the Planck Scale:**
   - At very small scales (like inside black holes or at the Big Bang), General Relativity breaks down, and quantum effects dominate—but we don’t have a complete framework (like a Theory of Everything) that unifies gravity with quantum mechanics.

4. **Whether Gravity Emerges From Something Deeper:**
   - Some theories propose that gravity is not fundamental, but **emergent**—arising from thermodynamics, information theory, or entanglement patterns (e.g., **Erik Verlinde’s entropic gravity**, or holographic principles).
   - These ideas are still speculative.

---

### So What *Is* Gravity, Really?
We don’t yet know **what gravity *is*** in the same way we understand, say, photons and electromagnetism. We have brilliant predictive models for *how it behaves*, but **the ontological root of gravity is still an open question**.

It’s one of the biggest frontiers in modern physics.
