Here’s a condensed, structured reference you can use to “speak the lingo” and understand the risks in BBM electrical systems:  

---

## **1. System Overview**
- **Service Entrance:** Medium voltage → step-down transformer → NEC Low Voltage (<600 VAC).
- **Main Switchgear:** Usually 3–4 per building, 2000–4000A service.
- **Distribution:** Internal busway feeds sub-breakers (HVAC, Lighting, MHE, etc.).
- **Key Risk:** Some loads connect to busways without intermediate ground fault protection — even small faults can trip an entire switchgear.

---

## **2. Main Breaker Trips**
**Causes:**
- *External:* Thunderstorms, downed lines, utility issues.
- *Internal:* Downstream ground faults, equipment failure.

**Trip Protections:**
- **Voltage Monitor:** ±8% of 480V 3-phase, 15s delay, 2-min reset.
- **Ground Fault Relay:** 4000A phase, 1200A ground pickup, 0.420 ms delay.

---

## **3. Safety**
- **Arc Flash Risk:** >100 cal/cm² possible; full PPE required.
- **Reset Rules:**  
  - Only by licensed electrician **after utility outage** per NA EHS policy.
  - Charging handle must be present.
  - Follow one-line diagrams and arc flash labels.

---

## **4. Troubleshooting & Reset Process**

### **Internal Problem**
1. Identify tripped breaker.
2. Verify building voltage.
3. If in tolerance → Arc flash PPE & barriers.
4. LOTO main breaker.
5. Shut all downstream breakers to 2nd level.
6. Check phase-to-ground resistance; isolate faulted circuits.
7. Re-energize main breaker with no load.
8. Restore downstream breakers one by one.
9. Repair and re-energize faulted circuits.

### **External Problem**
1. Identify tripped breaker & check building voltage.
2. If out of tolerance → Contact utility, notify Ops, start Sev 1/2 SOP.
3. PPE, barriers, all downstream breakers off.
4. After utility restoration → Re-energize main breaker no load.
5. Sequentially restore downstream breakers.
6. Inspect affected equipment & document.

---

## **5. >40 cal/cm² Breaker Work**
- **JHA Required**  
  - Create and maintain list of >40 cal/cm² equipment.
  - Assume unlabeled panels are >40 cal/cm².
- **SEV1–2 Ticket:** Submit to AMOC for tracking.
- **Utility Disconnect:**  
    - Arc flash/blast hazard at transformer.
    - Amazon personnel do **not** accompany utility during disconnect/reconnect.
- **Fault Investigation:**  
    - Outside contractor determines root cause before restoration.
    - Shed loads to the highest level PPE supports.
- **Verification:**  
    - Utility demonstrates zero volts on secondary side.
    - Contractor closes main breaker → Utility restores power.
    - Maintain arc flash boundary; ensure area is clear.

RME Base Building Management – Rapid Study Guide & Field Checklists

Purpose: Give you a fast, field‑ready playbook to breeze through the e‑learning and apply it on the floor the same day. Use this as your binder insert.



0) Universal Safety & Workflow (applies to all modules)

Golden rules: LOTO (no exceptions), energized work permits, hot‑work permits, confined‑space controls, ARC‑flash boundaries & PPE.

Always carry: Voltage detector, calibrated meter, radio, site maps, emergency contacts, permit pads, tags/locks, camera.

Documentation hygiene: Date every inspection, attach photos, record who/when, file in the right tab (see binder index at end).



1) Electrical Plan

Objectives - Read single‑line diagrams (SLD), know normal vs. emergency distribution, ATS/UPS path, breaker coordination. - Identify arc‑flash labels & boundaries; confirm panel schedules match reality.

Daily/Weekly - Visuals: Panel doors closed/latched, no obstructions, no missing knockouts, labeling clear. - IR scan cadence set; check GFCI/AFCI test schedule; verify e‑stops and EPO locations are known. - Trend generator/UPS events; review last alarms.

SOPs to keep handy - LOTO for panels/gear, energized work permit flow, portable generator tie‑in checklist.

Artifacts to collect - Latest SLDs, arc‑flash study, breaker coordination, panel schedules, ATS/UPS manuals, contractor service list.



2) HVAC Plan

Objectives - AHU/RTU anatomy: filters, coils, fans, VFDs, dampers; understand SAT/RAT/MAT, static pressure control, economizer logic. - Comfort & IAQ basics: setpoint ranges, deadbands, purge strategies, filter MERV, condensate management.

PM Cadence - Filters, belts, coil clean, VFD parameters backup, damper stroke tests, drain pan treatment.

BAS/BMS Focus - Trend key points (SAT, MAT, duct static, valve positions), alarm limits, time schedules, holiday tables.

Artifacts - Sequence of Operations (SOO), as‑builts, commissioning reports, PM checklists.



3) Fire Protection Plan

Objectives - Know system types (wet/dry/pre‑action, clean agent), FACP layout, detection zones, fire pump type. - Impairment protocol & hot‑work permitting.

Inspections/Tests (high level) - Fire pump test (per code & AHJ), valve status (tamper/supervisory), flow & alarm tests on schedule, extinguisher monthly checks.

Artifacts - Riser maps, FACP panel map, inspection tags/logs, impairment forms, vendor contacts & after‑hours numbers.



4) Dock Door Plan

Objectives - Door/leveler/restraint interlocks, signal lights, E‑stops, safety legs, pinch‑point awareness. - PIT (forklift) interface and safe approach/park procedures.

Daily Walk - Verify restraint indicators vs. door unlock logic; check leveler safety legs; inspect cords and lights; confirm bumpers & seals.

Artifacts - Interlock diagram, vendor PM schedule, incident/near‑miss log, parts list (springs, cables, pins).



5) Fuel Plan (Generators & Storage)

Objectives - Day tank vs. bulk tank, leak detection, containment, vents, fill/transfer; weekly exercise & run logs. - Know when spill plans and local permits apply.

Routine - Log fuel levels & water presence; verify battery chargers; inspect hoses/valves; schedule fuel polishing if needed.

Artifacts - One‑line for generator system, test logs, delivery SOP, spill kit locations, vendor list.



6) Elevator Entrapment

Objectives - Safe occupant communication, when to call the elevator vendor vs. first responders, basic controls at MRL/Control room.

Procedure (do‑not‑shortcut) 1. Establish communication; assess medical need. 2. Call contracted elevator tech; escalate to emergency services if needed. 3. Secure area; no self‑extraction unless trained/authorized jointly with vendor. 4. Document times, actions, and outcomes.

Artifacts - Call tree (vendor, security, site leadership), equipment IDs/locations, keys & access procedures.



7) Exterior Lighting Inspections

Objectives - Photocell/astronomical clock operation, pole/head condition, circuit protection, scheduled on/off alignment with hours of darkness.

Night Audit Steps - Walk/drive route map; note outages/flicker; check photocells; inspect pole bases for corrosion; GFCI where applicable.

Artifacts - Lighting circuit maps, control schedules, spare lamp/driver inventory, vendor bucket‑truck service.



8) RME Base Building Management System (BMS/BAS)

Objectives - Navigate graphics, trend critical points, tune alarms, and document setpoint changes with change control. - Understand BACnet objects, point naming/tagging standards, and basic PID behavior.

First Dashboards to Build - AHU summary (SAT, MAT, static, valve/damper position) - Space temperature heatmap by zone - Generator run/transfer history - Exterior lighting status & schedule adherence

Alarm Hygiene - Suppress noise, set sane deadbands/delays, route critical alarms to the on‑call correctly.
